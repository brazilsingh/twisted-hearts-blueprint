Twisted Hearts - the marketing blueprint

A shareable "relationship sin" quiz that turns strangers into an owned audience - and a $2.99 Prime rental into the start of a fanbase.

Film · Twisted Hearts (2026) Channel · Quiz → Email/SMS → Prime Status · Launch Ready
01 The strategy in one line

Sell the rental. Keep the audience.

Turn a confessional quiz into an owned list of relationship-content lovers, convert that list to a $2.99 rental on impulse - then keep them for everything Cue & Coda makes next.

At $2.99, you can't buy a customer. So the quiz lead - not the rental - is the asset. The rental is just the first thing you sell it.

The product nets roughly ~$1.50 per rental after Amazon's cut. No paid ad pays that back. That single fact decides everything: organic, viral, and creators do the heavy lifting, paid is a retargeting scalpel only, and SMS becomes your sharpest weapon - because a movie rental is a Friday-night impulse, and a text at 8pm converts impulse better than anything you own.

The asset

The list, not the rental

A relationship-quiz lead is worth far more than one rental over time - this film, the next one, merch, a sequel. Acquisition is justified against lifetime value, not $1.50.

The engine

The quiz spreads itself

"Every relationship has a sin - what's yours?" is an identity hook people click on themselves and tag others into. Every share is a free new entry to the funnel.

The closer

SMS on movie night

You already collect phone + SMS consent. A weekend-evening text to a warm lead is the highest-converting, lowest-cost move in the whole plan.

02 How it compounds

A loop, not a funnel

Most film marketing pours money in the top and hopes for sales at the bottom. This is built to feed itself: every result is content, every share is a new lead, every lead is kept.

Your job is to maximise three numbers - quiz completion, lead capture, and share rate. Get those high and growth becomes near-free.

03 The funnel, stage by stage

Six stages, one motion

STAGE 01

AttractGet people to the quiz

Don't advertise the movie cold - advertise the question. The quiz is the best ad you have.

  • Short-form video (TikTok / Reels / Shorts): read one provocative question, react to your result, dare the viewer to take it.
  • Creator seeding - your testimonial wall is full of actors who already love the film. Hand each their own "sin" card to post.
  • Quiz link in every bio. The quiz converts strangers; the homepage doesn't.
STAGE 02

CaptureQuiz → lead

Already well-built. Protect the conversion and push phone where you can.

  • Keep the email-or-phone choice - lower friction lifts capture.
STAGE 03

NurtureWhere low-ticket is won

A cold lead won't rent; a warmed one will. Run two parallel sequences.

  • Email (story): deliver the sin → "the couple in the film who shares it" → social proof + watch tonight → "send it to who needs it."
  • SMS (impulse): instant result link, then the Friday/Saturday 8pm date-night nudge.
STAGE 04

Convert→ Prime, $2.99

Win on timing, not discounts. Weekend evenings are your conversion window.

  • Deep-link straight to the Amazon Prime detail page — never a homepage in between.
  • Frame price as trivial, experience as shared: "less than a coffee, better than scrolling."
STAGE 05

AmplifyThe viral loop

Your cheapest growth - so engineer it deliberately.

STAGE 06

RetainMonetise the list

The rental is transaction one. The list is the business.

  • Tag every lead by their sin for segmented future campaigns.
  • Re-engage for the next release, behind-the-scenes, merch, or a sequel.

The rule: never send cold paid traffic to a $2.99 page. Cold traffic (if any) goes to the quiz, where the payoff is a lead - not a rental.

06 Measurement & the Amazon gap

Measure the click, infer the rental

⚠ Plan around this

Amazon PVOD gives filmmakers almost no purchase-level attribution. You cannot tie a specific lead to a specific rental.

So your north-star conversion is the Prime click-through - a trackable outbound click in GTM. Treat it as the proxy, then correlate aggregate Amazon sales reports against campaign timing to read true rental lift.

07 The first 30 days

Plumbing, then push

Week 01

Plumbing

Fix the live leaks below. Finalise email + SMS sequences. Confirm GTM events fire cleanly.

Week 02

Seed

Brief 10–20 creators with their own sin cards. Soft-launch to existing followers. Fix any quiz drop-off.

Week 03

Push

Creators post in a cluster. Run the seven-sins content daily. Turn on retargeting. First weekend SMS blast.

Week 04

Compound

Double down on the best two performers. Send the "send it on" email. Pull Amazon numbers against your Prime-click curve.

08 Priority fixes - live now

Quick wins plugging real leaks

Spotted on the live assets. Each is fast and pays back immediately.

FIX 01
Set a real page title.

The movie homepage title is currently New Page 1 - so every share on Facebook, iMessage and LinkedIn reads "New Page 1."

FIX 02
Deep-link the Prime button.

The quiz result's "Watch on Prime" link routes through a domain instead of the Amazon detail page - an extra bounce at the exact moment of intent.

The bottom line

Building a quiz instead of buying rentals is the right call for a $2.99 film. Win on completion, sharing, and SMS timing - and treat the list, not the rental, as the prize.
